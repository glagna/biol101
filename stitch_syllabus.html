Course Description

An immersive exploration into the biological mechanisms of light production in living organisms. From the deep-sea abyss to terrestrial fungi, students will analyze the chemical pathways of luciferin-luciferase reactions and their evolution across phyla. The course emphasizes direct laboratory observation and the potential applications of bioluminescence in modern biotechnology.

Learning Objectives

  • 01

    Identify and distinguish between biochemical markers of bioluminescence vs. fluorescence.

  • 02

    Model the evolutionary divergence of light-emitting structures in cephalopods.

  • 03

    Synthesize laboratory data from deep-sea sediment luminescence readings.

  • 04

    Analyze ethical implications of transgenic bioluminescent organisms.

  • 05

    Propose a novel application for bioluminescent sensors in human medicine.
