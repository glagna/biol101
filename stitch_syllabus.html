Course Description

A forum for the exploration of active research themes in the life sciences. Invited scientists from a range of universities and institutes present their current research and engage in discussion with seminar participants. This course gives students direct interactions with research academics in a range of fields, awareness of career opportunities, and contacts in those fields. This quarter features postdoctoral researchers and graduate students alongside established PIs, reflecting the full spectrum of scientific careers.

Learning Objectives

  • 01

    Read and critically analyze primary research papers across diverse biological disciplines

  • 02

    Formulate productive scientific questions using a structured question taxonomy

  • 03

    Engage directly with active researchers through seminar Q&A and discussion

  • 04

    Reflect on how different research approaches, methods, and career paths relate to your own interests

  • 05

    Develop professional skills through informational interviews and science communication practice

Course Structure

The quarter alternates between speaker weeks and activity weeks. Each speaker week follows a structured learning cycle: read the assigned paper, post a question to the Discussion Board, attend the seminar, and write a structured reflection. Activity weeks feature orientation, mid-quarter reflection, informational interview presentations, synthesis workshop, and final celebration.

Policies

gavel

Academic Integrity

Discussion questions and reflections must be your own work. Borrowing words from another student is a breach of academic integrity. Copying from the internet or published papers without proper referencing is also a breach. Use quotation marks and APA-style citations when quoting. If unsure about the integrity of any work, contact the instructor.

accessibility_new

Students with Disabilities

Students with disabilities should contact the Office of Accessible Education (OAE) to arrange accommodations. The instructor is committed to creating an inclusive learning environment. For any concerns, please reach out to Giorgio Lagna at glagna@scu.edu.

Career Inclusivity

Whether you're exploring academia, medicine, industry, policy, science communication, or something entirely different, you belong in this course. Your career path does not need to look like the speakers' paths. This seminar is designed to expose you to the full spectrum of scientific and research-adjacent careers.
