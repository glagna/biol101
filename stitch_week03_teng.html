Biography & Research Focus

Camilla is a postdoctoral researcher in the Bush Laboratory at UCSF's Department of Cell & Tissue Biology, specializing in the biomechanics of tissue morphogenesis. Her research investigates the fundamental cellular processes that drive tissue fusion—a critical step in preventing craniofacial defects like cleft lip and palate.

Recognized for her ability to communicate complex science, she was a finalist in the prestigious UCSF Postdoc Slam, where she translated her research into compelling narrative form. Her current work utilizes advanced live-imaging techniques to visualize how actomyosin contractility and cell adhesion coordinate tissue fusion in the developing face, with implications for understanding and potentially preventing birth defects like cleft lip and palate.

list_alt Glossary

Cellular Migration

Movement of cells through tissue, driven by cell-cell contacts and actomyosin contractility.

Tissue Fusion

Process by which two separate tissues merge into one, requiring coordinated cell migration.

Craniofacial Development

Formation of the head, face, and jaw during embryonic development.
