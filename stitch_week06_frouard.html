Study Guide: Key Concepts

HIV: Retrovirus attacking CD4+ T cells. Modern ART suppresses replication but cannot cure.

Latent reservoir: Cells with integrated viral DNA that don't produce virus.

Single-cell sequencing: DNA/RNA sequencing of individual cells.

HIV-seq: Tool for identifying HIV-infected cells.

Analytical sensitivity: Ability to detect rare events.

The Power of Good Questions

Use the question taxonomy below to frame your thinking. All four types of questions are valuable and welcomed:

Clarification

"What does [term] mean?" or "Can you explain [concept] in simpler terms?"

Method

"Why did you use [approach] instead of [alternative]?" or "How did you control for [variable]?"

Interpretation

"Could there be an alternative explanation for [finding]?" or "What about [confounding factor]?"

Extension

"What would happen if [scenario]?" or "How would this approach work for [related disease]?"

Don't worry about asking a "perfect" question. Researchers appreciate genuine curiosity and thoughtful engagement.

Post-Seminar Reflection

Write a short reflection (200–300 words) responding to both prompts below within one week.

1. Tool-Question Co-Evolution

Dr. Frouard developed a new tool to answer questions existing methods couldn't. Describe the problem she was solving and explain why existing tools were insufficient. What does this tell you about how scientific tools and questions co-evolve?

2. Ethics in HIV Research

HIV research carries significant social and ethical weight. What responsibility do researchers in this field have to affected communities? How might that shape how research is conducted and communicated?
