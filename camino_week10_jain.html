Study Guide

Key Concepts: Microglia, Neurogenesis & IGF1

Microglia: Resident immune cells in the brain. They play active roles in development and synaptic pruning. Neurogenesis: Birth of new neurons. Important for learning and memory. GABAergic neurons: Neurons using GABA as a neurotransmitter. Typically inhibitory. IGF1: Signaling protein that promotes cell growth and survival. Knockout model: Genetically engineered organism with a specific gene disabled.

Meet the Speaker

Training & Background

Dr. Jain is a neonatologist (physician specializing in newborn care) and postdoctoral researcher in the Piao Lab at UCSF. She maintains her own clinical practice in the NICU while pursuing research. Her path is distinctive: she completed medical training, entered clinical practice, then pursued research—a sequence that shapes how she frames scientific questions through a clinician's lens.

The Clinician-Scientist Path

Dr. Jain's dual identity—treating sick newborns and studying brain development—is central to her work. Her experience with infants at risk for neurodevelopmental complications directly informs her research on how microglia influence brain development. She represents one of the most challenging yet rewarding career paths: bridging clinical patient care and fundamental research.
