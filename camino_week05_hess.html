Pre-Class Preparation

This week's paper directly connects laboratory genomics to public health action. As you read, think about how sequencing data translates into real decisions that protect patients. Please complete these before Wednesday's session.

Primary Reading

Whole-Genome Sequencing for Monitoring Candida auris Outbreaks in Southern Nevada

Hess et al. (2023) • Frontiers in Public Health

This is the core paper Dr. Hess will discuss. It shows how genomic sequencing of a dangerous fungal pathogen helps public health officials track outbreaks and prevent spread. Focus on the abstract, introduction, and key findings. Pay attention to: What problem was Dr. Hess trying to solve? Why is Candida auris dangerous? How does sequencing data lead to public health action?

What You'll Learn

1

Genomic Surveillance in Practice

Understand how whole-genome sequencing is used to monitor infectious disease outbreaks and track pathogen spread in real time.

2

Lab to Public Health Translation

See how laboratory findings translate directly into public health decisions and infection control measures that protect communities.

3

Candida auris & Emerging Threats

Learn about a significant emerging pathogen, why it's dangerous, and how genomics is our best tool for controlling its spread.

4

Career Transitions in Biology

Hear directly from David about transitioning from academic faculty to public health—what that decision meant, and the rewards of research that impacts policy.
