Informational Interview Guide (Reference)

If you haven't completed your interview yet, here's the full guide (originally provided in Week 4).

How to Set Up an Interview

Step 1: Identify Your Person

Think about what interests you. Reach out to people you know, ask professors for introductions, or find people through LinkedIn.

Step 2: Write a Professional Email

Be specific, respectful, and brief. Say who you are, why you're interested in their work, and ask for 15–20 minutes.

Example opening:
"Hi [Name], I'm a student at Santa Clara University in a research seminar on biological sciences. I'm fascinated by your work in [specific area] and would love to learn about your career path. Would you have 15–20 minutes for a conversation?"

Step 3: Prepare Good Questions

Research them first. Prepare 5–7 open-ended questions like: "Can you walk me through your career path?" "What does a typical week look like?" "What surprised you most about your field?"

Step 4: Have the Conversation

Be on time. Listen more than you talk. Take notes. Be genuine. Ask follow-up questions. Thank them afterward with a brief email.
