school Background & Training

Dr. Vacante completed her post-doctoral fellowship at the Max Planck Institute for Heart and Lung Research, where she pioneered the use of deactivated CRISPR systems (dCas9) to modulate transcriptional activity in endothelial cells. Her work focuses on the intersection of epigenetics and non-coding RNA (ncRNA) biology.

Her current laboratory investigates how CRISPRa (activation) can be utilized to re-program vascular cells in patients with chronic ischemic heart disease, specifically targeting long non-coding RNAs that regulate angiogenic signaling pathways.
