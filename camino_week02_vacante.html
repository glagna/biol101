Study Guide

Glossary: Key Concepts & Technical Terms

Don't know a term? Start here.

cis-regulatory elements: DNA sequences that regulate genes in the same chromosome region.
lncRNA: Long non-coding RNA—RNA molecules >200 nucleotides that don't code for proteins but regulate genes.
miRNA: Small non-coding RNAs that typically repress genes by binding to mRNA.
CRISPRa: CRISPR activation—a modified CRISPR system that activates (turns on) genes without cutting DNA.
cis-ON system: Dr. Vacante's innovation: using RNA transcripts as recruiters to guide CRISPRa to target genes.
Cardiovascular disease: Diseases affecting the heart and blood vessels—a major focus of therapeutic research.

As you read the paper, jot down any other terms that confuse you. Bring them to class—asking "What does [term] mean?" is always a great question.

The Power of Good Questions

Use the question taxonomy below to frame your thinking. All four types of questions are valuable and welcomed:

Clarification

"What does [term] mean?" or "Can you explain [concept] in simpler terms?"

Method

"Why did you use [approach] instead of [alternative]?" or "How did you control for [variable]?"

Interpretation

"Could there be an alternative explanation for [finding]?" or "What about [confounding factor]?"

Extension

"What would happen if [scenario]?" or "How would this approach work for [related disease]?"

Don't worry about asking a "perfect" question. Researchers appreciate genuine curiosity and thoughtful engagement. If you say, "I'm confused about [topic]—can you help me understand it?" you've already started a productive conversation.

Post-Seminar Reflection

Within one week of the seminar, write a short reflection (200–300 words) on one of the prompts below. This consolidates your learning and helps you articulate what the research means to you.

Choose One Reflection Prompt

1. Key Insight

What was one key finding or concept from Dr. Vacante's research that surprised you or changed how you think about non-coding RNA? Explain what it means and why it matters.

2. Career Reflection

Francesca works at the intersection of fundamental research and translational medicine. Does that appeal to you? Why or why not? What aspects of her path resonated?

3. Science Question

What question about this research didn't get answered during the seminar? What would you want to investigate next, and how would you approach it?

A Note on Reflection

Reflections are not formal essays. Write conversationally, but thoughtfully. Be honest: if something confused you, say so. If the research didn't grab you, that's okay—explain what would have made it more engaging. The goal is to deepen your thinking, not to impress with jargon.
