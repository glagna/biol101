Welcome to BIOL 101

This interactive seminar brings you face-to-face with postdoctoral researchers, graduate students, and established scientists who are pushing the boundaries of biological research. Each week, a guest speaker presents their latest findings, shares their career journey, and engages with your questions in real time.

Your role is to come prepared, engage critically with research, ask thoughtful questions, and develop your own voice as a biologist. Whether you're exploring a career in research, medicine, industry, science communication, or beyond, this course is designed to help you see where your interests and strengths might lead.

How This Seminar Works

Each speaker week follows a structured learning cycle designed to help you engage deeply with cutting-edge research.

1

Prepare

Read the assigned paper, identify key concepts, and post a genuine question to the Discussion Board. This preps you to engage meaningfully with the speaker's research.

2

Experience

Listen to the speaker present their work, take notes, ask questions during Q&A. This is the heart of the seminar—real science, real researchers, real-time conversation.

3

Reflect

Write a structured reflection using rotating prompts that build in cognitive complexity. This deepens your understanding and develops your voice as a critical thinker.

4

Connect

Over the quarter, you'll see themes emerge across talks—different fields, methods, and career paths. Non-speaker weeks help you synthesize connections and clarify your own path.

A Note on What This Course Is (and Isn't)

This is an interactive seminar, not a lecture series. You'll engage directly with researchers, ask questions, discuss ideas, and develop your own voice. We assume you want to think about research, not just consume it.

All career paths are welcome. The speakers represent different career stages and pathways. Whether you're exploring academia, medicine, industry, policy, science communication, or something entirely different, this course is designed to help you see where your interests lead.

This is P/NP because engagement matters more than perfection. We're looking for genuine intellectual effort, not perfect answers. You pass by consistently participating in the learning cycle and showing up—literally and intellectually.
